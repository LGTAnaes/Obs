LGT Obstetric Anaesthesia

Quick Reference Guide

Regional Anaesthesia
Spinal
  • 14–16G IV access, warmed fluids running
  • 2.0–2.5 ml 0.5% heavy bupivacaine + 300 mcg diamorphine
    • Alternative: 100 mcg PF morphine + 10–15 mcg fentanyl
  • Phenylephrine infusion 50 mcg/ml started at 40 ml/hr
  • Aiming bilateral sensory block T4–S3
CSE
  • Consider for prolonged surgery or complex medical conditions
  • Reduce spinal dose in cardiac disease, short stature, obesity
  • Phenylephrine infusion 50 mcg/ml started at 40 ml/hr
  • Tegaderm dressing + Mefix strip
  • If initial spinal bolus does not achieve desired block height, use 5 ml bolus of epidural top-ups and re-assess
  • Consider 5–10 ml epidural top-up after 75–90 mins if surgery ongoing
Epidural Top-Up
  • Only if functioning adequately. If not, remove and place spinal
  • Use 1 of the following:
    • 0.5% bupivacaine
    • 0.75% ropivacaine
    • 2% lidocaine + adrenaline 1:200,000 (0.1 ml 1:1000 adrenaline drawn up to 20 ml with 2% lidocaine)
  • Test dose of 3 ml, followed by 5 ml increments up to a total of 20 ml
  • Add fentanyl 50–100 mcg to initial bolus
  • Diamorphine 3 mg (or PF morphine 3 mg) at end
Intra-operative pain under regional anaesthesia
  • If patient has epidural, a further top-up can be given
  • If no epidural, offer GA and convert if patient accepts
  • If GA declined, rescue analgesia includes:
    • Alfentanil 200 mcg increments, fentanyl 20 mcg increments
    • Ketamine 10–20 mg IV
    • Nitrous oxide in O₂ (50:50)
  • Clearly document the events and discussions with patient
General Anaesthesia
Indications
  • Category 1 emergencies
  • Neuraxial contraindicated:
    • Maternal refusal
    • Maternal coagulopathy
    • CVS instability
    • Maternal sepsis / localised infection
  • Regional anaesthesia inadequate for surgery
Management
  • 30 ml PO sodium citrate
  • 14–16G cannula with warm fluids running
  • Ramped position with Oxford HELP pillow
  • Suction switched on and accessible
  • Pre-oxygenation:
    • Pre-oxygenate with 100% O₂ via facemask
    • Consider HFNO during apnoea
  • When surgeon is scrubbed and ready, induce:
    • Propofol or Thiopentone
    • Rocuronium or Suxamethonium
    • Alfentanil up to 1 mg for hypertensive mothers
  • Videolaryngoscopy strongly recommended, size 7 COETT
  • Volatile agent immediately after intubation in 50% N₂O at high flows aiming MAC 1.0
  • Opioid analgesia following delivery (10–15 mg IV morphine) + PCA
  • Consider TAP blocks if patient consented (20 ml 0.25% bupivacaine each side)
Perioperative Medications for LSCS
Antibiotics (pre-incision)
  • Cefuroxime 1.5 g IV & Metronidazole 500 mg IV
  • Penicillin allergy: Clindamycin 600 mg IV & Gentamicin 4 mg/kg IV (max 400 mg)
Post-op Analgesia
  • Prescribe the 'C-section Medication Protocol' on iCare
    • Regular:
      • Paracetamol 1 g PO QDS
      • Ibuprofen 400 mg PO TDS
      • Morphine MR 10 mg PO BD (2 doses only)
    • PRN:
      • Morphine IR 10–20 mg 4 hourly
      • Ondansetron 4 mg IV 8 hourly
      • Cyclizine 50 mg PO/IV TDS
      • Naloxone 25 mcg SC hourly
Uterotonics
  • Elective:
    • Carbetocin 100 mcg IV
      • Caution in: asthma, CVS disease, hyponatraemia, migraine, epilepsy
    • If response inadequate, move to 2nd line agents
    • If carbetocin contraindicated: oxytocin 3–5 U bolus then 40-in-40 infusion @ 10 ml/hr
    • TXA 1 g IV for all patients
  • Emergency:
    • As per Elective uterotonics
    • Early movement to 2nd line agents as required
  • 2nd line agents:
    • Ergometrine 500 mcg IM
      • Avoid in PET, hypertension
      • Ensure antiemetics given
    • Carboprost 250 mcg IM
      • Avoid in asthma
      • Can repeat every 15 mins, up to max 8 doses
Trial of Instrumental Delivery
  • Anaesthetic as for LSCS as potential for conversion to LSCS
  • Regional anaesthetic block to T4
  • No routine prophylactic antibiotics
Manual Removal of Placenta
  • Assess CVS stability:
    • If unstable: general anaesthesia
    • If stable: neuraxial anaesthesia aiming for block to T6
      • Spinal: 2 ml 0.5% heavy bupivacaine ± fentanyl 10–15 mcg
      • Epidural top-up: bupivacaine, ropivacaine or lidocaine as per LSCS top-up — 10–15 ml
Perineal Tear Repair
  • Sacral block required
    • Spinal: 1.5–2 ml heavy bupivacaine ± fentanyl 10–15 mcg
    • Epidural top-up 10–15 ml
  • Remain sitting ~3 minutes to enhance sacral block
Cervical Suture Insertion / Removal
  • Block T10–S4 required
  • Spinal:
    • 2 ml heavy bupivacaine 0.5%
    • OR 2 ml hyperbaric prilocaine 2%
    • ± fentanyl 10–15 mcg
Epidural
Contraindications
  • Maternal refusal
  • Clotting abnormalities or thrombocytopenia
    • Platelets 75–100 ×10⁹/L — proceed if clotting studies normal
    • Platelets 50–75 ×10⁹/L — consider if benefits outweigh risk; most experienced operator
    • Platelets <50 ×10⁹/L — epidural contraindicated
  • Local infection at site of insertion
  • Allergy to amide LA
  • Lack of midwifery staff for 1:1 care
Procedure
  • L2/3, L3/4, L4/5
  • 16G Tuohy, LOR to saline
  • Leave 5 cm of catheter in the epidural space
  • Routine post-insertion checks (meniscal drop, aspiration)
  • Secure with Lockit, Tegaderm and Mefix dressing
  • Test dose 5 ml + 10 ml loading of low-dose bag mixture (0.1% L-bupivacaine + 2 mcg/ml fentanyl)
  • Maintenance infusion via Bodyguard pump
    • Code 611
    • PCEA with 8 ml (UHL) or 10 ml (QEH) bolus, 20 min lockout (max 120 ml / 4 hours)
Epidural Troubleshooting
No block or low block
  • 10–20 ml low-dose mix (bag solution) via epidural, re-check in 20 mins
  • If no improvement, resite
Missed segment
  • Truly a segment? vs unilateral block?
  • If confirmed, give 7–10 ml 0.25% bupivacaine + fentanyl 50 mcg
  • Position mother sore side down
  • If no improvement after 20 mins, resite
Poor sacral coverage
  • Suspect if describing groin, vaginal, rectal or perineal pain
  • Check S1/2 coverage with spray on lateral aspect of feet
  • Give 10–20 ml bag solution with mother sitting upright
  • Recheck after 20 mins; if not improved consider resite (ideally at lower interspace)
Unilateral block
  • Check if feet are warm and dry bilaterally
  • Lateral position sore side down, administer bolus from pump
  • If no improvement, pull catheter back by 1–2 cm and give another bolus
  • If still no improvement, give 7–10 ml 0.25% bupivacaine + 50 mcg fentanyl sore side down
  • Recheck block after 20 mins; if not improved consider resite
Lower back pain / rectal pressure
  • More likely in late stages of labour (8–10 cm dilation)
  • Particularly common with OP head position
  • Ensure block is working correctly (cold above T10, good sacral coverage)
  • Warn mother that complete relief may not be achievable
  • Give 7–10 ml 0.25% bupivacaine + 50 mcg fentanyl; sit mother upright and assess after 20 mins
Remifentanil PCA
Indications & contraindications
  • Use when epidural contraindicated or declined
  • Contraindicated: allergy to remifentanil, IUD, parenteral opioid within 4 hours, drug dependency, no trained midwife for 1:1 care
  • Discuss with consultant: pre-eclampsia, cardiac/respiratory disease, BMI >40, gestation <36 weeks, non-reassuring CTG
  • Currently available at UHL only
Administration
  • Dedicated 20G IV cannula, as proximal as possible
  • Continuous SpO₂ monitoring before starting and throughout
  • Remifentanil 2 mg in 40 ml 0.9% NaCl (50 mcg/ml)
    • Initial bolus 0.4 ml (20 mcg) over 15 sec, 3 min lockout
    • Increase to max 0.8 ml (40 mcg) if inadequate
  • Prescribe on iCare: ondansetron 4 mg IV 8 hourly PRN, naloxone 100–400 mcg IV PRN
  • Anaesthetist to assess for first 20 mins / 5 contractions
  • Stop and call anaesthetist: sedation score 3, RR <8, SpO₂ <94% on O₂, pump alarming — never flush line
Analgesia for labour after intra-uterine death
Approach
  • Discuss epidural and opioid PCA options with patient
  • Epidural preferred for longer, late-pregnancy labour
  • Morphine IV PCA preferred for shorter, earlier-pregnancy labour
  • Remifentanil PCA is contraindicated
  • Consider increased risk of sepsis and coagulopathy
Morphine IV PCA
  • Prescribe morphine PCA 1 mg/ml on iCare (standard theatre protocol)
  • Give initial dose of oral Oramorph or IM morphine
  • Prescribe: ondansetron 4 mg IV 8 hourly, cyclizine 50 mg IV PRN, naloxone 400 mcg IV, paracetamol + ibuprofen PO
  • 1:1 midwifery care; observations: RR, sedation, SpO₂, pain score
  • Anaesthetist to review regularly and follow up post-delivery

About this guide

This application is a quick-reference summary based on the LGT Obstetric Anaesthesia Guidelines.

It is a decision-support tool, but ultimate responsibility for treatment decisions remain with the responsible anaesthetist. It is intended for use by those trained in Obstetric Anaesthesia (post-IAOC) and does not replace clinical judgement or the full, formal guideline. This is not an MHRA determined medical device.
